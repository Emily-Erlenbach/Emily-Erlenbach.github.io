My name is Emily Erlenbach and I am a Data Scientist.

I completed my PhD in Kinesiology and Community Health at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) in May 2023. I was working with Prof. Neha Gothe in the Exercise Psychology Lab. My PhD thesis is entitled "One Step at a Time. Testing the Feasibility and Efficacy of a Novel Social Cognitive Theory-Based Intervention to Reduce Sedentary Behavior and Promote Physical Activity among Working Adults".

I also obtained my MSc in Kinesiology and Community Health at (UIUC) in August 2020. I was already working with Prof. Neha Gothe in the Exercise Psychology Lab. My Master thesis is entitled "The Association Between Light Physical Activity and Cognition among Adults: A Scoping Review".

Before starting my PhD at UIUC I worked for nine months at the GU Performance Lab in Berkeley, CA. During this internship I worked on Health Promotion and Exercise Physiology. I also performed literature reviews and exercise testings.

When I am not working, I am most likely training for my next triathlon or running race. I have completed twice the Davis Double Century bike ride!
